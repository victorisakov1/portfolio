Intro:

The problem of our business is to identify if sustainable wedding vendors are more cost effective. The main stakeholders are vendors and customers.

Sustainable Wedding Vendors - practice wedding activities/provide products and/or services that do less harm to ecology, people around, and support economy's vitality. (UCLA)

Being Cost Effective - providing good value for the amount of money paid.(Cambridge Dictionary, 2023)

How we measure Cost Effectiveness in our Wedding Database: we identify if customer satisfaction (Rating) by a product/service is worth the money paid. In our case, we compare if paying more (by customer) will provide them with a higher value. (Indeed, 2022)

Based on the definition of key terms, we can underline what we need to know: If sustainable vendors provide higher value for the same amount of money as non-sustainable vendors.And we will answer it by assessing relationship of Price of the products/services compared to Sustainable Practices as well as Rating of a product/service that vendors provide.

Answer:

To answer the business question, correlation between price per unit and sustainability has given the biggest answer of 0.15, which is considered a weak positive correlation. Further, when doing OLS model test of Vendor Rating to Vendor Sustainability, the results showed p-value less than 0.05 which suggested statistical significance of the relationship. In addition, I have completed another OLS model analysis between Price per Unit and Vendor Rating, which also had a p-value less than 0.05 and suggested a statistically significant relationship between these values.

It means, that increase in Rating leads to decrease in Sustainability. Also, increase in Price per Unit decreases Rating. And as we see in 2 histograms where Sustainable products/services are more expensive on average than Non-Sustainable ones, we can answer that Sustainable Vendors that sell sustainable products/services cost more than Non-Sustainable ones. And when they increase in price per unit, there is a correlation that customers will have a lower rating for the service/product they receive.

The outcome, Vendors with Sustainable practices are Not more cost effective due to lower customer satisfaction and higher price.

Top two actionable insights:

  1. Vendors should leverage information from this dataset and focus more on customer satisfaction which will provide them with higher returns and bring higher value to both - customers and vendors. Focusing on the quality of service/product rather than sustainability will lead to better results. (Gulanti and James, 2005) This dataset shows the affect of rating on cost effectiveness which should be utilized by vendors.

  2. Having lower prices for sustainable practices might increase customers' interest in getting that service and could impact more than just one company or a group of individuals. Decreased prices for Sustainable services will lower the threshold to entry by customers, which will lead to democratization of such service and potentially higher customer satisfaction and environmental impact. As price is considered a barrier for 41% of customers to buy a sustainable product. (Pieters et al, 2022) Dataset discovered higher price of Sustainable products/services which should be leveraged for vendors.
