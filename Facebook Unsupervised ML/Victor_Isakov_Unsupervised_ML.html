Intro¶

The following analysis discovered 4 main types of customers/business behaviors: Politically_Active, Pragmatists, Leads, and Young_Minds. Also, it discovered 3 Principal Components on Business Posts: Most_Popular_Brands, Common_Brands, and New_Brands. It also showcases how different groups are different, and that each of them should have a specific approach. Fashion businesses in Thailand that plan to utilize Photos in their Posts should look closer to the models 1 and 3 which have the best performance but have high False Positive predictions (predicting Photos when they are not) in about 20% of cases. The last Model (Model 3) shows best metrics of AUC (0.708) and Training gap of 0.005. The model is strong and will help to identify content that uses Photos and is targeting particular groups of clients. The business decisions of choosing the model would depend on the investment costs and how expensive it would be to under/over-predict.
If you care about missing less clients for Photo content, you should go with the Model 1, but if you care of the balance between these and would rather have less costs based on False Positives - go for the Model 3.
Based on the analysis, all three models scored close to each other and should be evaluated based on a particular business case.

Photos VS Other Content¶

Overall mean engagement of photos is around 203. Which means that on average a Photo post gets 203 reactions. For comparison, Videos get 5 times more average engagement which is about 1090 reactions per post. And Text posts get about 511 reactions per post on average. At the same time, Photos get about 175 likes per post, whereas Videos get about 257 likes, and Text posts - 467 on average. 
Moreover, likes is the most popular reaction on Photo posts which is also true for Text posts. But Video posts get more comments than anything else. Also, Shares are more popular in Video posts than any other content which drives engagement with the content and could possibly increase retention rate.
To compare, Photo posts get around 8 times more likes than comments, and almost 60 times more than shares. Other types of reactions are very rare on average.
Videos get about 674 comments on average (which is about 2.6 times more than they get likes), and 5.8 times more than shares.
And Text posts get about 12 times more likes than comments and have insignificant number of shares.

In addition, as mentioned previously, Video content gets the most engagement, highest number of Loves, Wows, and Hahas than other content. It could support the idea that videos drive customers to purchase or at least engage with the account content which drives retention.(Agarwal,2024)

def scree_plot(pca_object, export = False, cutoff=None):
    # building a scree plot

    # setting plot size
    fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(10, 8))
    features = range(pca_object.n_components_)

    variance_explained = pca_object.explained_variance_ratio_

    # developing a scree plot
    plt.plot(features,
             pca_object.explained_variance_ratio_,
             linewidth = 2,
             marker = 'o',
             markersize = 10,
             markeredgecolor = 'black',
             markerfacecolor = 'grey')


    # setting more plot options
    plt.title('Scree Plot')
    plt.xlabel('PCA feature')
    plt.ylabel('Explained Variance')
    plt.xticks(features)

    if export == True:
    
        # exporting the plot
        plt.savefig('./analysis_images/top_customers_correlation_scree_plot.png')
    
    
    plt.axvline(x=cutoff + 1, color='red', linestyle='--')  # +1 because component indexing starts at 0
    plt.text(cutoff + 1, np.mean(variance_explained), f'Cutoff line at Component {cutoff + 1}', color='red', ha='right')
    #I have used ChatGPT on the 2 lines above to create a vertical cutoff line.
    
    # displaying the plot
    plt.show()

def scaler(df):
    """
    Standardizes a dataset (mean = 0, variance = 1). Returns a new DataFrame.
    Requires sklearn.preprocessing.StandardScaler()
    
    PARAMETERS
    ----------
    df     | DataFrame to be used for scaling
    """

    # INSTANTIATING a StandardScaler() object
    scaler = StandardScaler(copy = True)


    # FITTING the scaler with the data
    scaler.fit(df)


    # TRANSFORMING our data after fit
    x_scaled = scaler.transform(df)

    
    # converting scaled data into a DataFrame
    new_df = pd.DataFrame(x_scaled)


    # reattaching column names
    new_df.columns = list(df.columns)
    
    return new_df

Model Selection¶

Model 3 (Retained Clusters) is the best choice due to a few factors.

1. It has the best AUC score which is above 0.7 (which is highest throughout models) and gives a better accuracy. It means it can better classify potential photo or video/text.

2. Model shows a low Train-Test gap which means that the model can be applied to a larger dataset to perform similar tasks. It will help to understand if the customer prefers photo or video/text.

Overall, the model shows a good accuracy of understanding which posts and which "segments" would be better suited for photos and which ones for videos.

In addition, the confusion matrix throughout all models have more or less similar outputs but the Model 3 shows the lowest False Positives. It means that if a certain Fashion Brand in Thailand decides to implement a strategy of only photos - they would invest less money into certain customer targeting (based on this model vs others), as the False Positives is lowest. False positives suggest that the model predicted that a post will be photo (or a customer would prefer photo) in under 20% of cases, where in reality, they would prefer Videos/Text.
The other Error - False Negatives - suggests that in 3.5% of posts, the model predicted other types of content where in reality they were photos. It means that the business would miss only on 3.5% of possible clients when investing in Photo content.

The outcome of the model is good but doesn't mean this is the best model to follow. The costs of investments should be evaluate before pursuing with it. Because on some case overestimation would lose more money than underestimation. And in other cases, loosing potential clients would be more dangerous to the business.
